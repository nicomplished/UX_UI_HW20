U.S. Space Force

Website Redesign

THE PROBLEM: The current Space Force website’s outdated visuals and cluttered information architecture makes it difficult for users to find the information they need.

THE SOLUTION: A minimalistic, but impactful redesign of the homepage and navigation.

MY ROLE: UI designer

PARTNER: Sanjay Roy for the User Research portion

TOOLS: Figma, Google Docs, Miro

Research Stage

Sanjay and I began our research by looking at the current United States Space Force (USSF) website and developed two user personas based on people who might want to visit this site. We then created a wireflow and a heuristic evaluation before redlining some features and pain points of the current sites that we saw.

After looking at the site with our own eyes, we came up with a User Testing Script to see if our users could locate specific pages or items, such as USSF Bases and Officers, facts, how Space Force impacts you, and how to join Space force.

User Testing

We each conducted five user tests to get potential users’ points of view. Our results showed that some of our users were unable to complete the tasks we asked them to with ease, or at all.

After testing, we organized our findings into a prioritization matrix to better guage the importance of certain features to our users.

What Changed?

After the User Testing stage, Sanjay and I went our separate ways to redesign the site.

I reorganized the sitemap by card sorting, created a new navigation system, redesigned the home page for both the mobile and web versions, and established a style guide for the site.

After testing and re-testing each version with over 11 different participants, my redesign of the home page was finished!

What’s Next?

If I were to continue working on the redesign for the United States Space Force, I would finish up the other internal pages for both mobile and web, continue with user testing, and possibly even reach out to the USSF for their opinions.
